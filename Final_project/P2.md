## Overview

The Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect describes a well-documented phenomenon where cities are significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas. In this project you will use Google Earth Engine (GEE) to access Landsat 8/9 Land Surface Temperature (LST) data and NLCD land cover data, map UHI across the Chicago metropolitan area, and quantitatively compare temperatures across different surface types.

## Part 1 — Background

### Landsat Collection 2 Level-2

Landsat Level-2 products have already been atmospherically corrected. The key band is `ST_B10` — Land Surface Temperature stored as a scaled integer.

> **LST Formula:** LST_Kelvin = ST_B10 × 0.00341802 + 149.0 → LST_Celsius = LST_Kelvin − 273.15

### Cloud Masking with QA_PIXEL

The `QA_PIXEL` band encodes quality flags as a bitmask. Use `bitwiseAnd()` to extract individual bits. Bits relevant for cloud masking: Bit 1 = Dilated cloud, Bit 3 = Cloud shadow, Bit 4 = Cloud, Bit 5 = Snow/Ice.

### NLCD Land Cover

NLCD 2021 is a 30 m land cover product available in GEE as `USGS/NLCD_RELEASES/2021_REL/NLCD`. Simplify to six groups: Water (11), Developed (21–24), Forest (41–43), Grassland/Shrub (52, 71), Agriculture (81, 82), Wetland (90, 95).
